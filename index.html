About Me

Hi! I’m Maggie, a second year undergraduate studying EECS and Physics at MIT. My interests lie in AI, quantum computing, and photonics. Right now, my research focuses on leveraging AI for scientific discovery in photonics. Looking ahead, I’m excited to dive into fields like physics-informed neural networks, hardware architecture for deep learning, edge AI, and quantum machine learning. I’m originally from sunny San Jose, California, where I developed my love for chess, long walks outdoors, and movie nights with friends and family.
Always curious, always exploring!

Research

I've been working in the Photonics and Modern Electromagnetics Group under Professor Marin Soljacic at MIT since September 2023. Currently, I'm creating an "AI Scientist" powered by a RAG LLM to automate the research process, specifically in topological photonics. I'm focusing on novel idea generation – getting the agent to generate innovate and feasible research ideas on par with those ideated by experts in the field. My other ongoing research project that we are writing up for publication is in photonic crystals; we designed a 3d photonic crystal that can confine light without a bandgap, instead with a bound state in the continuum.
